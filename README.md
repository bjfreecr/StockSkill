# Supply Chain Bottleneck Hunter

The methodology is distilled from Serenity's deep-dive approach to AI semiconductor supply chains, where he identified the InP substrate bottleneck (AXTI) years before it appeared on the White House shortage list and became a US-China trade negotiation item. This skill generalizes that same forensic process to any sector — solid-state batteries, HBM memory, hydrogen fuel cells, quantum computing, satellite internet, or whatever industry you name.

## How It Works

Six phases, executed in strict order:

1. **Define the end product** — subsystems table + demand growth context
2. **Reverse-map the supply chain** — layer-by-layer tree from raw materials to finished goods
3. **Bottleneck scoring** — four-axis scoring grid (supplier count, switching cost, certification cycle, technical barrier); only nodes scoring ≥10 advance; equipment suppliers with <100 global units get a +2 override
4. **Technical deep-dive** — source verification via patents, academic papers, industry reports, CEO earnings calls, and trade press
5. **Target screening** — filters to publicly traded pure-play companies only; adds market cap, institutional ownership, certification stage, and financial health
6. **Investment analysis output** — structured bottleneck cards with "Strait of Hormuz" analogies, catalyst timelines, what's-priced-in assessment, and bear case

## Output

Each bottleneck is delivered as a card containing:

- Supply chain score and tier (🔴 Critical / 🟡 Potential)
- Chokepoint analogy ("This is the Strait of Hormuz for...")
- Public company candidates with ticker, market cap, price, YTD, cert stage
- Independent source verification with quotes
- Catalyst timeline
- Institutional ownership trends
- Bear case and risk factors
